## Scope

In scope:

- Produce or reuse a redacted render pack of repaired `/flight-learn` focused cards after local-LLM-authored card-copy integration.
- Include model-enabled/draft cards and deterministic fallback cards where useful.
- Cover repeated workflow, validation/build, stale edit, low-information, safety/adversarial rejection, expected-known/expected-unknown, and evidence-summary cases where available.
- Capture actual operator-facing comprehension notes per card:
  - What happened?
  - Why does it matter?
  - Route or observe/no-artifact decision?
  - Confidence: clear / mostly clear / unclear.
  - Blockers, misleading wording, missing evidence, or privacy/safety concerns.
- Compute gate status using the 80% clear-or-mostly-clear threshold and zero safety/privacy blockers.
- Preserve evidence and privacy scans.

Out of scope:

- Source implementation changes. If validation identifies source/UX/model bugs, route a follow-up implementation ticket.
- Raw private Pi sessions, raw local paths, secrets, prompts, transcripts, stack traces, or unredacted local artifacts in Loom.
- Treating the operator's review choice as an actual artifact application.
- Dogfood corpus/outcome collection, classifier automation, route ranking, release readiness, or broad Bonsai quality claims.

Likely first Ralph run:

- Read this ticket, parent plan, runtime replay dossier, repaired render artifacts, and predecessor validation packet.
- Prepare an operator review packet if one is not already generated by the replay ticket.
- Stop honestly with `operator-review-pending` if actual operator notes are unavailable; do not fabricate them from worker judgment.

Stop conditions:

- Stop if representative cards are not available from the repaired renderer.
- Stop if validation would require raw private sessions or unredacted local artifacts.
- Stop if operator notes are not available; record `operator-review-pending` rather than closing.
- Stop if cards are too confusing in operator notes; route back to UX/model repair instead of corpus collection.

## Acceptance

- ACC-001: Representative repaired render pack is available.
  - Evidence: artifacts include at least 8 representative cards, or all available safe cards if fewer exist, covering model-enabled/draft, fallback, repeated workflow, validation/build, stale edit, low-information, safety/adversarial, expected-known/unknown, and evidence-summary shapes where available.
  - Audit: challenge sample breadth and whether the cards reflect repaired product rendering.

- ACC-002: Actual operator comprehension is measured.
  - Evidence: per-card operator notes answer what happened, why it matters, route/observe/no-artifact decision, confidence, and blockers. Worker precheck, schema validity, model output, or replay metrics do not count as operator comprehension.
  - Audit: challenge whether notes show understanding or only restate card text.

- ACC-003: Gate decision is explicit before corpus collection.
  - Evidence: dossier states proceed, repair model/card copy, repair fallback/inbox UX, or rerun validation. Proceeding requires at least 80% clear/mostly-clear with zero safety/privacy blockers.
  - Audit: challenge threshold math, ignored unclear cards, and safety exceptions.

- ACC-004: Evidence/provenance behavior is assessed.
  - Evidence: notes distinguish whether hidden evidence improves focus without blocking routing, and whether expansion remains sufficient when the operator needs provenance.
  - Audit: challenge whether evidence was hidden so well that trust or routing was weakened.

- ACC-005: Privacy and side-effect boundaries hold.
  - Evidence: privacy scan over artifacts/evidence/ticket, no raw private sessions/paths/secrets/prompts/transcripts, no source/storage/routing mutations, and no model text persisted as truth.
  - Audit: challenge accidental leakage and premature dogfood/corpus unblocking.
